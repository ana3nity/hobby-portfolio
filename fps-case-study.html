Improving the FPS Patch Notes Feature

Games Research & UX Design (GRUX) Project

The Problem

Patch notes are often separated from games, players have to read long text on separate browsers.

Hypothesis

By integrating a simplified patch feature in the Landing Pages will improve player engagement.

Solution

Adding a simplified patch note feature will increase players’ awareness of changes to weapons and other important items essential for gameplay performance by which will help the company build better communication to its players.

Starting with a Proto-Persona

Pain Points

  • When she hears weapon updates from friends too late
  • Doesn’t want to read long patch note updates
  • Triple-checking her loadouts to balance the stats of her weapons because certain weapons got nerfed/buffed

Goals

  • Get up-to-date with patch notes so she doesn’t fall behind during team plays
  • Know the updates that affect her performance the most
  • Know immediately when patch updates happen and which ones

Competitor Analysis

Conducting a thorough Comp Analysis that lasted for a little over a month due to learning a very thorough way to conduct a Comp Analysis in the Gaming Industry

Summary of Process

  • I recorded myself playing 3 FPS games on Deathmatch
  • First Recorded Playthrough: I played a full game
  • Second Recorded Playthrough: I explored the loadout feature

3 games I chose:

  • Call of Duty: Modern Warfare
  • Counterstrike: Global Offensive
  • Rainbow Six Siege

Breakdown of Competitor Analysis

The Competitor Analysis took me about almost 2 months to complete. It was iterated 3 times from honest feedback from my mentor.

I was also doing a lot of self-study by catching up on UX Summit videos to better understand the trends and evolution of UX in the Gaming Industry.

Even though it was a really heady process, I learned how to effectively analyze other games to draw design opportunities to become the foundation of my wireframes.
