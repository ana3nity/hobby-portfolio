kin games

Family-Owned Gaming Cafe Website Redesign & Redevelopment

See Live Sites

The Problem

Users’ Issue: Not understanding what is kin games is and struggling to build enough interest to make a visit
Business KPI: Increase public’s awareness and findability of the gaming cafe.

My Solution

Recreate a transparent & communicative website that satisfies users’ curiosities in order to increase foot traffic to the physical venue.

Overview

My Contributions

  • UX Research: Research Creation Plan, Proto-Persona, User Interviews (3), User Persona
  • UX Design: Heuristic Evaluation, Competitive Analysis, Storyboard, Site Map, Wireframes & Prototype
  • UI Design: UI Style Guide
  • Web Dev: Front-End Implementation

Duration of Project

  • 2 weeks
  • Solo Project

Design Goals

  • Revamp their website to increase users’ curiosity to raise conversion rates and retention on the website

Tools Used

  • Figma
  • Usability Hub
  • GitHub Pages

Insights from User Interviewees

Quotes & insights from User Interviews that helped me understand what users look for in a gaming cafe website.

“The website looks way too simple.”

“The only photo does not scream ‘Gaming Cafe’ to me, it looks like a corporate office.”

“If this was a gaming cafe...wish the name was ‘kin gaming cafe’ or ‘kin gaming something’...all the website says is kin and I don’t know what that means.”

Design Opportunities arose:

  • Clarifying the business' purpose
  • Users wanted more relevant, updated content of visuals
  • More information about provided services to get a sense of what kin offers

Takeaways

Key Learnings

  • Internal operations conflicts affect design decisions.
  • Small staff of 3 leading to limitations in committing to entirety of project.
  • Not having a cohesive business brand confuses stakeholders, designer, and customers.
  • Small-Businesses need to pay attention to current digital marketing trends; an SEO-friendly website will significantly increase the findability on search engines by people who enjoy Board & PC games.

Challenges

  • Design conflicts with kin’s developer and technicalities with GitHub Pages ultimately leading to a lot of push back.
  • Revamp of website was not a high priority; business led by 3 staff members who wear ‘many hats’.

The Impact

  • Foot traffic has steadily increased over Summer 2023.
  • Increased communication between customers and staff. Higher rates of people wanting to reserve PCs and Events.
  • The website was made accessible with the QR Code to the website to people passing by the venue.
  • People without Social Media accounts and messaging platforms such as Discord were able to access the website to easily see information.
