kin games

Family-Owned Gaming Cafe Website Redesign & Redevelopment

See Live Site

The Problem

Users’ Issue: Not understanding what is kin games is and struggling to build enough interest to make a visit
Business KPI: Increase public’s awareness and findability of the gaming cafe.

My Solution

Recreate a transparent & communicative website that satisfies users’ curiosities in order to increase foot traffic to the physical venue.

Overview

My Contributions

  • UX Research: Research Creation Plan, Proto-Persona, User Interviews (3), User Persona
  • UX Design: Heuristic Evaluation, Competitive Analysis, Storyboard, Site Map, Wireframes & Prototype
  • UI Design: UI Style Guide
  • Web Dev: Front-End Implementation

Duration of Project

  • 2 weeks
  • Solo Project

Design Goals

  • Revamp their website to increase users’ curiosity to raise conversion rates and retention on the website

Tools Used

  • Figma
  • Usability Hub
  • GitHub Pages

Insights from User Interviewees

Quotes & insights from User Interviews that helped me understand what users look for in a gaming cafe website.

“The website looks way too simple.”

“The only photo does not scream ‘Gaming Cafe’ to me, it looks like a corporate office.”

“If this was a gaming cafe...wish the name was ‘kin gaming cafe’ or ‘kin gaming something’...all the website says is kin and I don’t know what that means.”

Design Opportunities arose:

  • Clarifying the business' purpose
  • Users wanted more relevant, updated content of visuals
  • More information about provided services to get a sense of what kin offers

Breakdown of Research

Discovering Insights from Stakeholders

Summary of their Org Goals:

  • Increase donations
  • A section that highlights their 50th anniversary coming up soon in October 2023
  • A website that reflects Filipino culture; grassroots with a professional edge

Applying Insights to the Designs

Helped the design team further realize that the improved Information Architecture needs to have the ‘Donate’ button visible to potential donors.

Recreation of the Site Map

In order to resolve the navigational issues on the website, the UX Design team worked together to decide on making a navigation bar from scratch by rearranging the original site map.

Usability Testing

2 rounds of Usability Testing (5 participants each) to validate the User Flow of Users finding Mental Health Resources on FAJ’s Website

*Conducted by the UX Program Design Team (Panda Ninjas)

Results: Both users of FAJ’s website & stakeholder were satisfied with the new layout and UI Style Guide of the High-Fidelity Prototype. Wordpress Implementation and website launch was scheduled in June 2023.

Usability Testing Revisited

After recruiting new UX Designers, we picked up from where the 1st Design Team last left off in the Summer of 2023.

Results: Both users of FAJ’s website & stakeholder were satisfied with the new layout and UI Style Guide of the High-Fidelity Prototype. Wordpress Implementation and website launch was scheduled in June 2023.

High-Fidelity Wireframes

My wireframe contributions were:

  • Domestic Worker/Caregiver
  • Kapwa Ko (Worker Support Group)
  • Civic Engagement
  • Get Involved

Takeaways

Key Learnings

  • Being a apart of 2 design teams; transferring work to another team with a 2 month gap period; finding and recruiting UX Designers to continue project.
  • Transitioning into a Project lead role for the 2nd team.
  • 1st paid UX project; battling perfectionism & understanding the need to compromise & negotiate.
  • ULearning curves of learning low-code websites such as Wordpress and only using free plugins - stakeholders expressed limited budget.
  • Working with stakeholders that don’t understand technologies very well; delivering information in a way that doesn’t overwhelm them.

The Impact

  • Within the first 2 weeks, FAJ received $500 donation for their 50th Anniversary and 2 contacts interested in becoming members.
  • As the 50th Anniversary in October 2023 is coming up, FAJ is steadily garnering interested volunteers and 50th Anniversary donations.
