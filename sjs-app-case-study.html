San Jose Strong App

Volunteer-run Organization connecting residents to resources in South Bay

See Live App (Will Launch Jan 2024)

The Problem

Since 2020, their app has been developed & revised at least twice. Due to lack of volunteers specialized in UX, the design process was put on the back burner.

Our Solution

Introduce the importance of including UX Research + UX Design Methods to Software Engineers in order to help the organization recreate a user-centric app with a clear purpose to users that also causes significantly less rework for developers.

Overview

My Contributions

  • UX Research: Research Creation Plan, Proto-Persona (3), User Interviews, User Persona
  • UX Design: Heuristic Evaluation, Competitive Analysis, Storyboard, Site Map, Wireframes & Prototype

Duration of Project

  • April - August 2023

Team

UX Team of 4 Volunteers

  • 1 Lead UX/UI Designer
  • 2 UX Designers (including me)
  • 1 UI-focused Designer

Tools Used

  • Figma, AdobeXD
  • Usability Hub, Calendly, Maze
  • Fonts from fontawesome
  • App created with JS React Native for iOS & Android users

Main Challenges

  • Working asynchronous with UX Team led to long dead periods and miscommunications
  • Biggest Challenge: Advocating for the design process to finish first before the App Dev Team starts; introducing agile sprint methods

How I Overcame Them

  • Gave regular feedback/presentations to UX Team & Developers to break down what is UX Research the principle of “test early, test fast” in UX Design
  • I recruited 2 other UX Designers to help SJS’s sole UI/UX Designer to speed up the project timeline

How User Research strengthened the new designs

User Persona

  • The discovery of a user persona brang clarity to the UX Team, Web Dev Team, App Dev Team, and SJS Stakeholders about the App’s focus and refined user flows that aligned with SJS’ Mission Statement.

Target Demographic

People who are:

  • New and curious about South Bay area and/or
  • Would like to know existing community-oriented groups and resources local to them

*Photo from Unsplash.com

How User Research strengthened the new designs

Competitive Analysis

  • Comp Analysis was conducted twice to improve the Low-Fidelity wireframes of 2 user flows for my portions to Mid-Fidelity (Events Page and Orgs & Businesses Page)

Main Takeaways synthesized from Strengths and Weaknesses from 3 mobile applications were beneficial for the UX Team and SJS Stakeholders to understand what modern, tried-and-true features can be used to rework the 2022 AdobeXD Prototype version.

Iteration Process

  • 5 moderated Usability Tests from Mid-Fi to the final Hi-Fi
  • An additional 2 unmoderated Usability Tests were conducted to collect more qualitative data to discover more design pain points; recruiting & scheduling became difficult due to potential participants finding zoom interviews time too involved and elaborate

*Photos used in wireframes are from Unsplash.com

Suggested UI Style Guide

  • According to usability testing feedback, majority of users felt the aesthetics of the Mid-Fidelity designs looked "outdated and dull".
  • The UX Team came up with a brighter color palette that gave a more modern, refreshing, inviting, and exciting feel.

Takeaways

Key Learnings

  • In the end of the project, despite the UX team reporting back all user research findings to improve the user flows and UI Style Guide Accessibility, the stakeholders ultimately wanted to keep to their vision of the app.
  • Attempting to change a fixed, siloed work culture is tough. Advocating design thought processes & the importance of UX Research to budding UX teams and Web/App Devs can be challenging but in the long-term, extremely rewarding by walking away with knowing which battles to fight in future UX projects.
  • Team misalignment causes miscommunications and affects the overall quality of the wireframes and prototypes. Due to different communication styles and schedules, synergy with the UX Team was disjointed at times. Lags in soft deadlines occurred, delaying the ability to make faster team decisions about design direction adjustments. Fostering & prioritizing unities over differences with great patience, empathy, & facts-based reasoning were hard-fought. Thus, ultimately achieving harmony within the team during isolated moments.

Trade-Offs:

  • The developers + members of SJS learned how to integrate empathy as they build the website and app.
  • Changing wireframes is much faster than changing code
  • Usability Testing with non-members of SJS proved to the developers that we get objective, less-biased testing results

The Impact

  • Vastly upgraded user flows within the design after synthesizing all data from User Interviews and Stakeholder Interview
  • UX/UI Teammates identified visual accessibility issues within the previous designs
